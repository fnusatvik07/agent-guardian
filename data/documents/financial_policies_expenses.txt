Financial Policies and Expense Management

This document outlines financial policies, expense management, and procurement procedures.

EXPENSE REIMBURSEMENT POLICY:
- Submission Deadline: Expenses must be submitted within 60 days of incurrence
- Required Documentation: Original receipts for all expenses over $25
- Approval Limits: 
  * Up to $500: Direct manager approval
  * $501-$2,500: Department head approval
  * Above $2,500: Finance team and VP approval
- Reimbursement Timeline: Approved expenses paid within 10 business days
- Mileage Rate: Current IRS standard rate ($0.655 per mile for 2023)

TRAVEL AND ENTERTAINMENT:
- Flight Booking: Economy class for domestic, business class for international flights over 6 hours
- Hotel Accommodations: Standard business hotels, $250/night limit in major cities
- Meal Allowances: $75/day domestic, varies by international location
- Ground Transportation: Ride-sharing or taxi receipts required for trips over $25
- Client Entertainment: Business purpose required, alcohol limits apply
- Personal Expenses: Not reimbursable (personal calls, minibar, movies)

PROCUREMENT PROCEDURES:
- Purchase Orders Required: All purchases over $1,000
- Vendor Approval: New vendors require finance and legal review
- Contract Terms: Standard payment terms are Net 30
- Budget Allocation: Purchases must have approved budget allocation
- Competitive Bidding: Required for purchases over $10,000
- Emergency Purchases: Retroactive approval process for urgent needs

CREDIT CARD POLICIES:
- Corporate Cards: Available for employees with regular travel/expense needs
- Spending Limits: Based on role and historical expense patterns
- Personal Use: Strictly prohibited, may result in immediate card cancellation
- Lost/Stolen Cards: Report immediately to finance team and card provider
- Statement Review: Monthly reconciliation required within 15 days
- Violations: Repeated policy violations may result in card revocation

BUDGET MANAGEMENT:
- Annual Planning: Department budgets due October 1st for following fiscal year
- Quarterly Reviews: Budget vs actual analysis with variance explanations
- Budget Transfers: Require CFO approval for transfers over $5,000
- Capital Expenditures: Assets over $5,000 require depreciation planning
- Project Budgets: Separate tracking for initiatives with dedicated funding
- Contingency Planning: 5% buffer recommended for unexpected expenses

FINANCIAL REPORTING:
- Monthly Closes: Department expense reports due by 5th of following month
- Accruals: Estimate unpaid invoices for month-end reporting
- Cost Center Allocation: Shared expenses allocated based on usage metrics
- Variance Analysis: Explanations required for variances over 10% or $1,000
- Forecasting: Quarterly updates to annual forecast based on trends
- Key Metrics: Track spend per employee, budget utilization, and cost trends

INVOICE PROCESSING:
- Invoice Submission: Send to accounts.payable@company.com
- Three-Way Match: Purchase order, receipt confirmation, and invoice required
- Approval Workflow: Electronic routing based on dollar amount thresholds
- Payment Terms: Take advantage of early payment discounts when beneficial
- Vendor Communications: Direct all payment inquiries to AP team
- Document Retention: 7-year retention for all financial documents

FINANCIAL CONTROLS:
- Segregation of Duties: No single person handles entire transaction cycle
- Bank Reconciliation: Daily cash position monitoring and monthly reconciliation
- Petty Cash: $500 maximum, receipts required for all disbursements
- Check Signing: Dual authorization required for checks over $5,000
- Wire Transfers: CFO approval and verification required for all wires
- Audit Compliance: Maintain documentation to support annual audit requirements

TAX COMPLIANCE:
- Sales Tax: Automated calculation and filing for all jurisdictions
- Income Tax: Quarterly estimated payments and annual filing
- Payroll Tax: Timely deposit and reporting for all payroll obligations
- International: Transfer pricing and foreign tax credit considerations
- Documentation: Maintain supporting records for all tax positions
- Professional Services: External tax advisor for complex transactions
